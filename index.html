01 — Problem & solution
A preventable crisis.
An unsolved problem.
Doctors prescribe dozens of medications daily. No tool today combines instant interaction checking, AI-predicted side effects, and personalised patient context in one simple, affordable interface.
Problem space: drug safety AI · ML · healthcare Primary users: doctors & pharmacists Market: clinical decision support
The problem

Patients often take multiple medications simultaneously, dramatically increasing adverse interaction risk. Today's workflow is broken:

  • Doctors rely on memory or scattered, outdated databases
  • Interaction checks are time-consuming and interrupt clinical flow
  • No tool personalises risk based on patient age, conditions, or history
  • Harmful combinations go undetected until a patient is already harmed
The solution

MediSafe AI gives healthcare professionals instant, personalised drug safety intelligence at the point of prescription:

  • Detects drug–drug interactions in under 2 seconds
  • Predicts likely side effects with AI-ranked severity scoring
  • Personalises risk based on age, gender, and existing conditions
  • Surfaces actionable recommendations — not just raw warnings

05 — Core features
What the product does
Six features — prioritised by user impact and build complexity. Features 1–3 are MVP scope. Features 4–6 are Phase 2 and beyond.
Feature 01 · MVP · P0
Drug interaction checker
Identifies harmful drug combinations instantly. Provides severity classification — Critical, High, Medium, Low — with a plain-language explanation of the mechanism of harm.
Must have — core product value
Feature 02 · MVP · P0
AI side effect predictor
Predicts the most likely adverse reactions for a drug combination, ranked by probability and severity. Goes beyond known interactions to surface probable but less-documented risks.
Must have — key differentiator
Feature 03 · MVP · P0
Risk scoring system
Assigns a composite risk score (0–100) combining interaction severity, side effect probability, and patient vulnerability. Helps doctors prioritise which alerts need immediate action.
Must have — enables triage
Feature 04 · Phase 2 · P1
Patient personalisation
Adjusts risk predictions based on individual patient profile — age, weight, kidney/liver function, and existing conditions. Turns a generic warning into a patient-specific alert.
Important — drives retention
Feature 05 · Phase 2 · P1
Smart alerts dashboard
A doctor-facing dashboard showing all active high-risk alerts across their patient list, recent checks, resolved alerts, and trend data. Enables proactive monitoring, not just reactive checking.
Important — hospital adoption driver
Feature 06 · Phase 3 · P2
EHR system integration
API integration with Epic and Cerner to embed MediSafe AI directly into existing hospital prescription workflows. No tab switching — the check happens inside the tool doctors already use.
Future — enterprise growth lever

09 — Key product decisions & trade-offs
What we chose
and why
Every product decision involves trade-offs. Here's what was prioritised in MVP and the reasoning behind each choice.
Decision 01
Simplicity over accuracy in MVP
✓ Chosen: Speed & usability first
A more accurate deep learning model would take 6+ months to train. Speed to validate the concept with real users matters more at MVP stage. Accuracy improves in Phase 2 with real-world hospital data.
Decision 02
Public datasets over proprietary data
✓ Chosen: FDA FAERS & DrugBank
Proprietary clinical data is expensive and requires regulatory agreements. Public datasets are immediately available, well-validated, and sufficient for MVP. Hospital data is incorporated in Phase 2 via pilots.
Decision 03
Web app before mobile app
✓ Chosen: Web app for MVP
Doctors primarily use desktop in clinical settings. A responsive web app reaches both desktop and mobile without the cost of two native apps. A dedicated mobile app comes in Phase 3 for the patient persona.
10 — Risk register
What could go wrong
Five real risks — rated by severity with a clear mitigation strategy for each. No product is risk-free; what matters is knowing the risks and planning ahead.
Risk Impact Severity Mitigation
Model predicts an incorrect safe interaction Patient harm — the highest stakes failure mode Critical Position as decision support, never autonomous. Require doctor confirmation for all High-risk flags. Clear disclaimer on all outputs. Validate against gold-standard clinical databases before any launch.
Regulatory classification as medical device FDA / CE mark requirements could delay launch 1–2 years High Early consultation with regulatory experts. Initial positioning as a "clinical reference tool" (lower burden) rather than diagnostic software. Legal review before MVP launch.
Low adoption due to workflow friction Product fails to gain traction despite good technology Medium Co-design MVP with 5–10 doctors through user interviews and prototype testing before build. Freemium model removes cost barrier. Hospital pilot programme drives institutional adoption.
Drug database becomes outdated Inaccurate interaction data — missed or false alerts Medium Automated weekly sync with FDA FAERS and DrugBank. Internal data quality monitoring with alerts when source databases update. Manual clinical review for flagged edge cases.
Patient data privacy and HIPAA compliance Data breach — legal liability and complete trust destruction Medium MVP does not store any patient identifiable data. All processing is session-based and ephemeral. Full HIPAA compliance architecture designed before Phase 2 patient personalisation launches.
